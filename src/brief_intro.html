Wen Xia is a Professor at the School of Computer Science and Technology, Harbin Institute of Technology, Shenzhen. He received his Ph.D. degree in Computer Science from Huazhong University of Science and Technology in June 2014. From 2011 to 2013, he was a Visiting Scholar at the University of Nebraska, USA, and a Research Assistant at NEC American Labs. His research interests include storage systems, data deduplication, data compression, operating systems, file systems, etc. His papers have been published in top-tier conferences and journals, including OSDI, FAST, ASPLOS, USENIX ATC, EuroSys, etc. He has been the recipient of numerous awards, such as the USENIX FAST Distinguished Artifact Award (2025), the ACM SIGOPS China Outstanding Doctoral Dissertation Award (Advisor, 2023), the ACM SIGOPS China Rising Star Award, and the ACM TOS Distinguished Reviewer Award (2021). He currently serves as an Associate Editor for IEEE Transactions on Computers (2026–Present). He is also a PC Member for several premier Systems conferences, including FAST, EuroSys, Storage, etc. He is a member of ACM, CCF, IEEE, and USENIX.
